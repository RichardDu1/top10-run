## Overview

AI-driven cash flow forecasting combines historical transaction data with real-time external market signals (e.g., interest rates, GDP, industry trends) to predict future cash positions with high accuracy. This guide walks through setting up an automated forecasting pipeline using enterprise-grade tools.

## Prerequisites

- Access to bank accounts, ERP, and accounting software (AP/AR)
- Historical cash flow data (at least 12 months)
- API access for external data sources (e.g., economic indicators)

## Step 1: Data Aggregation

Connect your financial systems to a centralized platform like **Kyriba** or **Treasury AI**. Use pre-built connectors for:
- Bank accounts (real-time balances)
- Accounts receivable aging
- Accounts payable schedules
- Payroll data

## Step 2: Integrate External Market Signals

Configure data feeds for external signals such as:
- Central bank interest rates
- Industry-specific indices
- Macroeconomic indicators (GDP, inflation)

Use APIs from providers like Bloomberg or Refinitiv.

## Step 3: Train AI Models

Leverage the platform's machine learning engine to train models on historical data. Set parameters:
- Forecast horizon (e.g., 30, 60, 90 days)
- Confidence intervals
- Anomaly detection thresholds

## Step 4: Automate Forecasting

Schedule daily or hourly model runs. The AI will:
- Update predictions as new data flows in
- Flag anomalies (e.g., unexpected large payments)
- Adjust forecasts based on external signals

## Step 5: Review and Act

Use dashboards to visualize forecast vs. actual. Set alerts for:
- Cash shortfalls
- Excess liquidity
- Significant deviations from predictions

Take actions like adjusting credit lines or investment strategies.

## Best Practices

- Regularly retrain models with new data
- Validate forecasts weekly against actuals
- Combine AI insights with treasury team judgment
