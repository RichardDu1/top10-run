## Overview

This workflow guides you through implementing an AI-driven inventory classification and reorder optimization system. By analyzing historical demand patterns, you'll categorize items using ABC-XYZ analysis and set dynamic reorder points to minimize stockouts and overstock.

## Prerequisites

- Access to historical sales data (at least 12 months)
- Inventory management software with API or export capability
- Basic understanding of inventory metrics (turnover, lead time, safety stock)

## Step 1: Data Collection and Preparation

Export historical demand data from your inventory system. Ensure the dataset includes:
- Item SKU
- Date of transaction
- Quantity sold
- Unit cost
- Lead time (from supplier)

Clean the data by removing returns, cancellations, and outliers. Use a tool like **Zoho Inventory** to automate data export via its REST API.

## Step 2: ABC Classification Based on Revenue Contribution

Calculate annual revenue per SKU (quantity sold × unit cost). Sort items descending by revenue and assign:
- **A items**: top 20% contributing ~80% of revenue
- **B items**: next 30% contributing ~15% of revenue
- **C items**: bottom 50% contributing ~5% of revenue

Use **NetSuite AI Inventory** to automate this classification with its built-in ABC analysis module.

## Step 3: XYZ Classification Based on Demand Variability

Compute the coefficient of variation (CV) for each SKU: CV = (standard deviation of demand) / (mean demand). Classify:
- **X items**: CV < 0.5 (stable demand)
- **Y items**: 0.5 ≤ CV < 1.0 (moderate variability)
- **Z items**: CV ≥ 1.0 (erratic demand)

**Inventory Planner** can automatically calculate CV and generate XYZ categories from historical data.

## Step 4: Combine ABC and XYZ into a Matrix

Create a 3×3 matrix combining both classifications. For example:
- **AX**: high revenue, stable demand → low safety stock, frequent review
- **CZ**: low revenue, erratic demand → minimal stock, reorder only on demand

Use the matrix to define different replenishment strategies per segment.

## Step 5: AI-Powered Demand Forecasting

Feed historical demand into an AI forecasting model. Tools like **NetSuite AI Inventory** use machine learning to predict future demand considering seasonality, trends, and promotions. Generate forecasts for each SKU for the next 3-6 months.

## Step 6: Dynamic Reorder Point Calculation

For each SKU, calculate reorder point (ROP) using:
ROP = (Average daily demand × Lead time) + Safety stock

Safety stock is determined by service level target (e.g., 95%) and demand variability. Use AI to adjust safety stock dynamically based on forecast uncertainty. **Inventory Planner** provides automated ROP calculations with AI-driven safety stock optimization.

## Step 7: Set Reorder Quantities (EOQ with AI)

Compute Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) for each item, but enhance with AI to account for demand variability and supplier constraints. **Zoho Inventory** offers AI-assisted order quantity suggestions that balance holding costs and ordering costs.

## Step 8: Implement and Monitor

Upload the new classification and reorder parameters into your inventory system. Set up automated alerts for low stock and review the matrix quarterly. Use dashboards in **NetSuite AI Inventory** to monitor service levels, inventory turnover, and stockout rates.
