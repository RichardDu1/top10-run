## Overview

This guide walks you through using AI-driven BOM optimization tools to reduce component costs while maintaining performance and mitigating supply risks. By the end, you'll have a repeatable workflow to generate cost-saving alternatives for your BOM.

## Prerequisites

- A current Bill of Materials in CSV or Excel format (columns: Part Number, Description, Quantity, Unit Cost, Supplier, Lead Time)
- Access to an AI BOM optimizer tool (we'll use **Wizerr AI BOM Optimizer** and **AI BOM Cost Analysis**)
- Basic understanding of component specifications

## Step 1: Prepare Your BOM Data

Export your BOM from your PLM or ERP system. Ensure it includes:
- Part numbers
- Descriptions
- Quantities
- Current unit costs
- Supplier names
- Lead times (if available)

Clean the data: remove duplicates, standardize units, and verify part numbers.

## Step 2: Upload BOM to AI Optimizer

Log in to **Wizerr AI BOM Optimizer** (https://wizerr.ai).
- Click "Upload BOM" and select your CSV file.
- The AI will parse and validate your data.
- Review the parsed BOM for accuracy.

## Step 3: Run Cost Optimization Analysis

- Click "Optimize Costs".
- Set parameters: target cost reduction (e.g., 15%), preferred suppliers, and acceptable lead time changes.
- The AI will search its database of millions of components and suggest alternatives.

## Step 4: Review AI Suggestions

The tool returns a list of alternative components with:
- Part number and description
- Estimated cost savings
- Performance impact (e.g., same specs, slight tolerance change)
- Supply risk score
- Supplier details

Filter by savings percentage or risk level. For each alternative, click "View Details" to see datasheets and cross-reference.

## Step 5: Validate with Engineering Team

- Export the shortlisted alternatives to a shared spreadsheet.
- Use **AI BOM Cost Analysis** (https://aibomcostanalysis.com) to run a second opinion on pricing and availability.
- Cross-check critical parameters (voltage, temperature range, etc.) with your design team.

## Step 6: Approve and Update BOM

- For approved alternatives, update your BOM in the PLM system.
- Set a re-evaluation schedule (e.g., quarterly) to catch new cost-saving opportunities.

## Best Practices

- Always verify AI suggestions with actual supplier quotes.
- Combine cost savings with lead time improvements.
- Use the AI's supply risk score to avoid single-source dependencies.

## Troubleshooting

- If the AI returns few alternatives, broaden your search parameters (e.g., allow equivalent parts from different manufacturers).
- For missing part numbers, manually add them to the tool's database via the "Add Component" feature.

## Conclusion

By integrating AI BOM optimization into your workflow, you can consistently reduce material costs by 15-20% while maintaining product quality and supply chain resilience.
